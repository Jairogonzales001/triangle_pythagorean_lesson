Triangle Inequality Theorem

The sum of ANY two sides must be GREATER than the third side.

a + c > b
b + c > a

All THREE conditions must be true!

üîë Key Takeaways

Triangle Inequality: Add the two smaller sides - if the sum is GREATER than the longest side, a triangle CAN be formed.
Quick Check: Just check if the sum of the two shorter sides > longest side. If this fails, no triangle is possible!
Pythagorean Converse: Square the two shorter sides, add them. Compare to the square of the longest side.
Classification: Equal = Right triangle, Greater = Acute triangle, Less = Obtuse triangle

Remember the Order!

1. First: Can these sides form ANY triangle? (Triangle Inequality)

2. Then: What TYPE of triangle? (Pythagorean Converse)
